What is IndieWebClub?

IndieWebClub is part of a growing world-wide network of meetups for everyone who wants to take back their web experience from social media silos, and own their online identities & content, or just want support with blogging!

  • 📝 Want to blog more and looking for: ideas, momentum, and a supportive community?
  • 📅 Join a gathering of like-minded people and get friendly support with writing, creating, and anything related to using and improving your own website!
  • 👩🏽‍💻 You do not need to have any tech background or experience in having your own website. You can even start with a Google Doc or your local notes app!

The IndieWebClub Bangalore community organizes in-person meetups at a roughly two-week cadence in the city of Bangalore, India.

You can join us on WhatsApp or Discourse. You can follow our blogroll and event updates on Mastodon. You can also reach out to us via email.

Agenda

Our agenda this time is anonymity. What’s the point of publishing anonymously, what effect that has on our work, and how can we accomplish that on the IndieWeb?

  • What’s the purpose of being anonymous when you publish online? What are the costs to being anonymous?
  • Why publish anonymously at all? After all, you might not gain anything by doing so.
  • What is the effect of writing under a pseudonym on writing quality or audience trust?
  • If a pseudonym is popular enough, is it same as a real name?
  • How can we be truly anonymous on the IndieWeb when so many services have our private data? When LLMs can unmask us by analyzing our writing? Is it even possible? In light of that, can the IndieWeb be a safe space for dissent?
  • What’s the difference between anonymity and privacy?
  • Are there different levels to anonymity? Can you be sort-of-anonymous without completely hiding your identity?

We’ll spend the last hour of the meetup writing and publishing a piece on our personal websites. We’ll provide writing prompts to get you started.

Remember to bring a device you can use to write and post to your blog!
